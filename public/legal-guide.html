Avoid Legal Problems

GetVideosLocally is a multi-site downloader for 1000+ supported sites. With that reach comes responsibility. This guide helps you use it legally and ethically.

What You CAN Download

Creative Commons Content

Videos explicitly licensed under Creative Commons (CC) licenses. The creator has given permission for downloading and sharing.

Your Own Content

Videos you created and uploaded yourself. You own the rights and can download them anytime.

Content with Permission

Videos where the creator has explicitly given you permission to download (in writing is best).

💡 Pro Tip: Look for videos labeled "Creative Commons" or "Free to Use" in the description. Many creators explicitly encourage downloading.

Be Careful With

Standard Licensed Content

Most YouTube videos use "Standard YouTube License." Downloading for personal offline viewing may be acceptable in some regions, but redistribution is NOT allowed.

⚠ Important: Even if you download for "personal use," you should NEVER:
  • Re-upload to any platform
  • Use in your own videos without permission
  • Share publicly or sell

Never Do This

  • Download and re-upload someone else's content as your own
  • Sell or monetize downloaded content without rights
  • Use copyrighted music/videos in commercial projects
  • Mass-download entire channels without permission
  • Circumvent paywalls or member-only content
🚫 Consequences: Copyright infringement can result in DMCA takedowns, account termination, and legal action including fines.

YouTube License Types

Standard YouTube License

This is the default license for YouTube videos. The uploader retains all rights. YouTube's Terms of Service allow viewing/streaming but do not grant download rights. Whether personal downloads are acceptable depends on your local laws (some countries have "private copying" exceptions).

Creative Commons CC BY License

Videos with this license can be freely downloaded, shared, and even modified—as long as you give credit to the original creator. Look for the "CC" badge under the video.

How to check: On YouTube, click "Show More" under the video description. The license type is shown at the bottom.

Other Websites

GetVideosLocally works with 1000+ supported websites, including but not limited to YouTube. Remember:

  • Each website has its own Terms of Service
  • Each video may have different licensing
  • Always check the specific rights before downloading
  • When in doubt, ask the content creator directly

Quick Reference

  • Download your own content anytime
  • Download Creative Commons content (give credit)
  • Download with explicit creator permission
  • Standard license = personal use only (varies by region)
  • Never redistribute, sell, or claim ownership
